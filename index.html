History of Rolland Garros : the well-known tennis championship

The story begins in 1891 with the creation of the "French Championships on clay". Reserved for players registered in French clubs and played alternately at the Stade Français, Parc de Saint-Cloud, and the Racing Club de France, at La Croix-Catelan, they saw the hero of the time, Max Decugis, win 8 times between 1903 and 1914. A first major turning point came in 1925, when the event opened its doors wide to foreigners: the "Internationaux de France" were born. On the court, it was the golden age of French tennis. On the women's side, Suzanne Lenglen triumphed 6 times between 1920 and 1926. She is the first real tennis star. Her male counterparts Henri Cochet, René Lacoste, Jean Borotra and Jacques Brugnon, the famous Musketeers, compiled 10 singles titles between 1922 and 1932. Above all, their victory in the Davis Cup in 1927 led to the construction of a stadium dedicated to the defence of their title: it was named after Roland Garros, the pioneer of aviation who died in combat during the First World War.
